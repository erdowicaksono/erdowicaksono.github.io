In Indonesia

Pura Besakih

Pura Besakih is a significant and revered Hindu temple complex located on the slopes of Mount Agung, the highest volcano on the island of Bali and is the largest on the island.

Candi Borobudur

Borobudur, is a remarkable Buddhist temple complex located in Central Java, Indonesia. Recognized as one of the most impressive and important Buddhist monuments in the world.

Gong Cave

Gong Cave, is a unique and fascinating cave located in Pacitan, East Java, Indonesia. The cave is renowned for its natural beauty, unique rock formations, and the mesmerizing sound when struck, produce musical tones resembling the sound of a gong.

Indonesia culture

Introduce Indonesia culture

Tari Pendet is a traditional Balinese dance that originated from the island of Bali, Indonesia. This dance is a sacred and ritualistic dance often performed as an offering to the deities during temple ceremonies.

Reog Ponorogo is a traditional Indonesian art form originating from Ponorogo, a regency in East Java, Indonesia. It is a unique and vibrant cultural performance that combines dance, music, and elaborate costumes, featuring a spectacular mask and a large, intricate peacock-like headdress.

Kecak Dance is a traditional Balinese dance that is unique in its use of vocal chants as the primary musical accompaniment. This dance form originated in the 1930s and is closely associated with the Balinese culture, often performed as part of cultural events and ceremonies.
